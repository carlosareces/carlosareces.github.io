Escuela de Lingüística Computacional 2011

1-4 de Agosto 2011, Córdoba, Argentina

Submissions

In a change from previous AiML's, there will be two types of papers:

  • (1) Full papers for publication and presentation at the conference.
  • (2) Abstracts for short presentation only.

Both types of paper should be submitted electronically using the submission page at

http://www.easychair.org/AiML08/

The online submission system will be opened a few weeks before the submission deadline of 31 March 2008.

(1) Full Papers

These will be published by College Publications

http://www.collegepublications.co.uk

in a volume to be made available at the meeting. Authors are invited to submit for review a full paper, not submitted elsewhere. It should be at most 15 pages plus optionally a technical appendix of up to 5 pages, together with a plain-text abstract of about 100-200 words.

To appear in the conference volume, papers must be prepared in LaTeX using the style files and following the instructions provided below. At least one author of each accepted paper must register for and attend the conference to present the paper.

(2) Abstracts

These should up to about 5 pages. They may describe preliminary results, work in progress etc., and will be subject to light review. They may be made available at the conference, and authors should indicate if they would like to make a short presentation of their abstract of up to 15 minutes.

Instructions for Authors

Full paper authors are invited to submit a paper of at most 15 pages plus optionally a technical appendix of up to 5 pages, formatted according to the AiML LaTeX style available here, together with a plain-text abstract of about 100-200 words. Please use the following template for your LaTeX file.

Authors of accepted papers are not requested to transfer the copyright of their papers. However, they agree to grant Advances in Modal Logic Initiative a royalty free non-exclusive license to publish the paper in full in the Advances in Modal Logic Series, and to be posted on the World Wide Web on the Advances in Modal Logic Initiative server (aiml.net).
